Idli Recipe (With Homemade Idli Batter)

                    		1. Ingredients for Idli:
  • For Idli Batter:
    • 2 cups of idli rice
    • 1 cup of urad dal (black gram)
    • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
    • Salt to taste
  • For Idli:
    • Idli batter
    • Water for steaming
    • Idli molds or plates

2. Instructions for Idli Batter:
  1. Rinse the idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under running water.
  2. Soak the idli rice and fenugreek seeds together in water for 4-6 hours.
  3. Soak the urad dal in water for 4-6 hours as well.
  4. Grind the soaked urad dal and rice separately to a smooth batter using a little water as needed.
  5. Mix the ground urad dal and rice batter together, adding salt. Allow it to ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  6. The next day, the batter should have risen and become light. Mix it well.

3. Instructions for Idli:
  1. Grease the idli molds or plates with a little oil.
  2. Pour the idli batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full.
  3. Steam the idlis in a steamer for 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  4. Allow the idlis to cool for a few minutes before removing them from the molds.

4. Ingredients for Sambar:
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • Vegetables (carrots, drumsticks, potatoes, onions, tomatoes, etc.)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Tamarind pulp (lemon-sized ball soaked in water)
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

5. Instructions for Sambar:
  1. Cook toor dal in a pressure cooker with enough water until it becomes soft and mushy.
  2. In a large pot, add chopped vegetables, chopped onions, tomatoes, tamarind pulp, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the cooked toor dal to the pot and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Allow them to splutter.
  5. Pour the tempering into the sambar and mix well. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
