                    		1. Ingredients for mendu Vada:
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, grated
  • A handful of fresh curry leaves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

2. Instructions for mendu vada recipe:
  1. Soaking Urad Dal: Rinse urad dal under cold water and soak it in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.

  2. Draining and Grinding: After soaking, drain the water from urad dal. Grind the dal in a blender or wet grinder, adding minimal water to form a smooth and thick batter.

  3. Adding Seasonings: Transfer the batter to a mixing bowl. Add finely chopped green chilies, grated ginger, chopped curry leaves, cumin seeds, and salt to taste. Mix well to incorporate the seasonings evenly.

  4. Shaping the Vadas: Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Keep a bowl of water handy. Wet your hands with water to prevent the batter from sticking. Shape the batter into round discs with a hole in the center.

  5. Frying: Carefully slide the shaped vadas into the hot oil. Fry on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp on both sides.

  6. Draining Excess Oil: Use a slotted spoon to remove the vadas from the oil. Place them on absorbent paper to drain excess oil.

  7. Serve: Medu Vadas are traditionally served with coconut chutney and sambar. They can also be enjoyed with tomato chutney or yogurt.

  8. Enjoy: Serve the Medu Vadas hot and crispy. The combination of the crispy exterior and soft interior makes them a delicious snack or breakfast item.


3. Ingredients for Sambar:
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • Vegetables (carrots, drumsticks, potatoes, onions, tomatoes, etc.)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Tamarind pulp (lemon-sized ball soaked in water)
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

4. Instructions for Sambar:
  1. Cook toor dal in a pressure cooker with enough water until it becomes soft and mushy.
  2. In a large pot, add chopped vegetables, chopped onions, tomatoes, tamarind pulp, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the cooked toor dal to the pot and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Allow them to splutter.
  5. Pour the tempering into the sambar and mix well. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
